# Declaration Zero - The Case for Change
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

## What This Framework Is

Groupthink is a framework for human coordination that grows from the ground up. The framework must work at small scale first and prove itself before it can credibly operate at large scale.

### A framework of emergent complexity

Groupthink is a framework that grows with you. A group of friends coordinating game nights and a federation of communities managing shared water rights both use Groupthink - but the complexity each encounters is different, because their needs are different.

The framework is complete at every stage of use. A group that coordinates only its own decisions is using the framework fully. As a group's needs grow, the framework grows with it - by activating principles that were always present.

### The Core and the functions

**The Core** is the set of values and rights that define what a Groupthink group is. Every group, at every scale, for every purpose, starts here. The Core establishes floors - hard limits that cannot be crossed - and directions - values that every group commits to moving toward, knowing they will never fully arrive. *(See Documents/Core/)*

Beyond the Core, the framework provides additional values, rights, and implementation guidance that activate based on what a group *does*:

- **Steward** - when a group manages shared resources, natural or built. *(See Documents/Stewardship/)*
- **Federate** - when a group relates formally to other groups. *(See Documents/Federation/)*
- **Provide** - when a group supplies essential needs to its members. *(See Documents/Provision/)*
- **Defend** - when a group protects against irreversible harm to persons or the commons. *(See Documents/Defense/)*
- **Account** - when a group holds persons or groups accountable for harm. *(See Documents/Accountability/)*

These are functions - things a group does - and each brings additional responsibilities. Some functions operate within a group; others - federation, mutual defense, accountability - operate between groups, coordinating responses that no single group can handle alone. A group's profile is simply a description of what it does: "We coordinate decisions and steward a community garden." No ceremony. No rank.

### Values as directions with floors

The values in this framework are directions. We move toward maximum consent, minimum coercion, maximum transparency, maximum equality. Every decision can be evaluated: did this move us toward or away from these values?

A small number of values are absolute - floors that cannot be crossed regardless of circumstance. These floors exist because some harms cannot be undone. *(See Documents/Core/0.Values.md)*

## What This Framework Offers

It offers one form of governance among many. If it is better, people will choose it. If it falls short, it should be abandoned or improved.

It promises to be honest about what it can do, and to grow only as fast as its principles can sustain.

It assumes people are capable of coordinating with each other, and that when they can't, they are free to walk away. It also assumes that some people will act in bad faith, and that governance must be robust to this - through structural accountability, through mutual defense, and through making cooperation so valuable that defection costs more than it gains.

A Groupthink group can be five friends deciding what to do on Friday. The framework meets you where you are.
