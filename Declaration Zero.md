# Declaration Zero - The Case for Change
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## The World As It Is

We begin with what is plainly visible.

### Power concentrates

Capital and political power flow upward. The mechanisms designed to distribute power - markets, elections, institutions - have been captured by the people they were meant to restrain. Wealth builds on wealth. Influence buys more influence. The result is a world where a shrinking number of people make decisions that shape the lives of everyone else.

This has always been true. What has changed is the scale. Global systems of finance, technology, and governance have made concentration faster, harder to see, and harder to resist than at any point in history.

### Growth cannot be infinite

The dominant economic model requires endless growth on a finite planet. It treats the living world as raw material and waste as someone else's problem. The atmosphere, the oceans, the soil, and the systems that sustain life are treated as externalities - things that disappear from the balance sheet until they collapse.

It is a physical contradiction. An economy that must grow forever in a world that cannot grow will eventually consume its own foundation.

### Representation is broken

Democratic systems were built to give ordinary people a voice in how they are governed. In practice, the distance between a person and the decisions that affect their life has grown so wide that the relationship barely functions. Representatives respond to donors, to media cycles, to party structures - to everything except the people they nominally represent.

The systems designed to channel participation have been hollowed out. Voting for a representative every few years falls far short of meaningful participation in the decisions that shape your daily life.

### The social contract is broken

When power concentrates, when growth is unsustainable, and when representation fails, the implicit agreement between people and their institutions breaks down. The promise - that if you participate, follow the rules, and contribute, the system will protect your interests - is no longer credible for most people.

People know this. The loss of trust in institutions, the rise of disengagement, the fracturing of shared reality - these are symptoms. The cause is that the contract has been violated by the people who hold the most power within it.

## What This Framework Is

Groupthink is a framework for human coordination that grows from the ground up. Over time, it can assume the functions currently performed by nation-states - by making them unnecessary. This will take generations. The framework must work at small scale first and prove itself before it can credibly operate at large scale.

### A framework of emergent complexity

Groupthink is a framework that grows with you. A group of friends coordinating game nights and a federation of communities managing shared water rights both use Groupthink - but the complexity each encounters is different, because their needs are different.

The framework is complete at every stage of use. A group that coordinates only its own decisions is using the framework fully. As a group's needs grow, the framework grows with it - by activating principles that were always present.

### The Core and the functions

**The Core** is the set of values and rights that define what a Groupthink group is. Every group, at every scale, for every purpose, starts here. The Core establishes floors - hard limits that cannot be crossed - and directions - values that every group commits to moving toward, knowing they will never fully arrive. *(See Documents/Core/)*

Beyond the Core, the framework provides additional values, rights, and implementation guidance that activate based on what a group *does*:

- **Steward** - when a group manages shared resources, natural or built. *(See Documents/Stewardship/)*
- **Federate** - when a group relates formally to other groups. *(See Documents/Federation/)*
- **Provide** - when a group supplies essential needs to its members. *(See Documents/Provision/)*
- **Defend** - when a group protects against irreversible harm to persons or the commons. *(See Documents/Defense/)*

These are functions - things a group does - and each brings additional responsibilities. A group's profile is simply a description of what it does: "We coordinate decisions and steward a community garden." No ceremony. No rank.

### Values as directions with floors

The values in this framework are directions. We move toward maximum consent, minimum coercion, maximum transparency, maximum equality. Every decision can be evaluated: did this move us toward or away from these values?

A small number of values are absolute - floors that cannot be crossed regardless of circumstance. These floors exist because some harms cannot be undone. *(See Documents/Core/0.Values.md)*

## What This Framework Offers

It offers one form of governance among many. If it is better, people will choose it. If it falls short, it should be abandoned or improved.

It promises to be honest about what it can do, and to grow only as fast as its principles can sustain.

It assumes people are capable of coordinating with each other, and that when they can't, they are free to walk away. It also assumes that some people will act in bad faith, and that governance must be robust to this.

A Groupthink group can be five friends deciding what to do on Friday. The framework meets you where you are.
