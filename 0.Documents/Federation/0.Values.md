# Federation - Values
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## When This Applies

A group **federates** when it relates formally to other groups - making agreements, coordinating activities, sharing resources across group boundaries, or building shared governance structures. This document describes the values that apply when groups relate to each other.

These values build on the Core (Documents/Core/0.Values.md) and extend it.

---

## The Principle

Groups interact. They share resources, coordinate activities, make agreements, and sometimes disagree. When groups relate to each other, a new set of values becomes relevant - distinct from the values that govern individuals within groups.

It names the values that must be honoured regardless of the form that relationship takes - whether groups choose to form federations, make bilateral agreements, share resources informally, or simply coexist.

---

## The Values

### Sovereignty

Every group governs itself. No arrangement between groups overrides a group's internal self-governance. A group may choose to coordinate decisions with other groups, but the choice must be continuous and revocable.

### Voluntary association between groups

A group's participation in any inter-group arrangement is always voluntary. A group may join, stay, or leave any arrangement at any time.

### No coercion between groups

No group may use its position or resources to pressure another group. Trust is structural - a group's record of keeping its commitments is legitimately relevant to other groups' willingness to cooperate. Punishing a group for exercising a right (leaving, splitting, dissenting) is coercion. Conditioning future cooperation on demonstrated reliability is a natural consequence of trust.

### Equal standing

In any arrangement between groups, each group has equal standing. Every group carries one voice, regardless of size, resources, or history.

### Bounded scope

Any arrangement between groups has a defined scope - the specific concerns it addresses. Decisions outside that scope have no standing. Scope expansion requires the consent of all participating groups.

### Transparency between groups

Groups in any arrangement have the right to see the full record of decisions made within that arrangement.

### Agreements are promises

An agreement between groups is a mutual promise. The consequence of breaking a promise is the loss of trust and the network consequences that follow. This is the natural structure of trust at scale.

### Independence of relationships

A group's relationships are independent of each other. Each relationship stands on its own. Every group's standing in one arrangement is independent of every other.
