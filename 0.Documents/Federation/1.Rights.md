# Federation - Rights
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## What This Is

These are the rights of groups in relation to one another. They apply whenever groups relate formally - through federations, compacts, agreements, or any other arrangement. They build on the Core rights (Documents/Core/1.Rights.md).

---

## Rights of Groups

### 1. Sovereignty

Every group governs itself. No arrangement between groups overrides a group's internal self-governance.

### 2. Voluntary participation

A group may join, stay, or leave any inter-group arrangement at any time. No group may be compelled to join or remain.

### 3. No coercion

No group may use its position or resources to pressure another group into any arrangement.

### 4. Equal standing

Each group has equal standing in any arrangement, regardless of size, resources, or history.

### 5. Bounded scope

Any arrangement has a defined scope. Decisions outside that scope have no standing. Scope expansion requires consent from all participating groups.

### 6. Transparency

Every position taken, every agreement reached, every disagreement recorded - visible to all participating groups.

### 7. The right to split

Any group may split at any time, for any reason. This right is unconditional. Every arrangement must allow splits freely. The right to *join* an arrangement after splitting is separate - arrangements may set conditions for membership.

### 8. Independent dispute resolution

When groups cannot resolve a dispute, any party may seek adjudication by people with no stake in the outcome. Adjudication findings are recorded. Compliance is voluntary; the finding stands in the record and other groups may act on it.

### 9. Independence of relationships

A group's relationships are independent. Each arrangement stands on its own. A group's compacts, memberships, and internal governance survive the end of any single relationship.

### 10. The commons limits all arrangements

No arrangement between groups can approve exceeding what the commons can sustain. Any stakeholder may invoke the commons against any inter-group decision. *(See Documents/Stewardship/.)*
