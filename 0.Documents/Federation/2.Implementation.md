# Federation - Implementation
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## What This Is

This document offers starting points for how groups can relate to each other within the Groupthink framework. It describes two forms - federations and compacts - and groups may relate in any way that honours the Federation values and rights.

---

## Federations

A federation is a group of groups that governs shared concerns through iterative deliberation.

### Forming and joining

1. Any two or more groups can form a federation. The founding groups agree on the federation's scope - the specific shared concerns it addresses. This scope is recorded.
2. A new group joins when a current member group vouches for it and the federation discusses. The prospective member should have a genuine stake in the federation's shared concerns.
3. Each member group has one voice, regardless of size.
4. A member group can leave at any time.
5. Scope is bounded. Expanding scope requires consent from all member groups.

### Voices

1. Each member group selects a voice - a person who participates in federation discussions on the group's behalf.
2. A voice carries the group's position. They may only commit to positions the group has agreed to.
3. A voice can be recalled at any time by their group.
4. Everything the voice says and does is recorded.
5. A group is only bound by positions it has consented to.

### The iterative process

Decisions emerge through iterative deliberation - positions flow back and forth between the federation and its member groups until agreement emerges or the matter is set aside.

1. Any member group can raise a concern within the federation's scope.
2. Groups deliberate internally to form positions.
3. Voices convene to present positions, listen, and surface disagreements. The purpose at this stage is understanding, and binding consent comes later.
4. Voices return to their groups. Groups run another internal discussion with the new information.
5. Iterate. Each round should narrow the gap.
6. When agreement seems close, any voice can call for a formal consent round.
7. If consent cannot be reached, the matter is set aside. Groups that agree can act together through a compact.

### Splitting within federations

1. Splitting is unconditional.
2. When a member group splits, neither fragment automatically keeps federation membership. Fragments apply through the normal process.
3. The federation should welcome fragments in good faith. Refusing a fragment to punish splitting violates the Core.

### Federations of federations

1. Federations can federate. At every layer, the same values apply.
2. Each layer is sovereign. Member governance stands at every layer.
3. Speed matches scope. Higher layers take longer. Deliberation should not be shortened past the point of genuine internal discussion.
4. Scope narrows at each layer. Higher layers handle only concerns that genuinely span all members.

---

## Compacts

A compact is a voluntary agreement between two or more groups - separate from a federation. It creates no governing body.

1. Any groups can form a compact. They discuss terms, consent, and record it.
2. A compact binds only the groups that consented.
3. Any group can leave a compact at any time.
4. Compacts rely on trust. A broken promise carries reputational consequences.
5. Compacts are visible - recorded in each group's record.
6. Compacts are independent of federation membership.

---

## Safeguards

1. Membership is voluntary and continuous.
2. No social coercion for choosing not to join or for leaving.
3. Federations should consider how decisions affect people and communities not represented by any member group.
4. The full federation record is available to all member groups at all times.

---

*This is the implementation document for Federation. It offers starting points for inter-group coordination. Groups may form federations differently, relate through compacts, or use structures yet to be imagined - provided the Federation values and rights are honoured.*
