# Stewardship - Values
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## When This Applies

A group **stewards** when it manages shared resources - natural or built. A shared garden, a community server, a tool library, a watershed, an energy grid. The moment a group takes responsibility for something that others depend on, stewardship begins.

This document describes the values that apply when a group stewards. These values build on the Core (Documents/Core/0.Values.md) and extend it.

---

## The Principle

We share a physical world. The air, the water, the land, the energy, and the living systems we depend on are finite, shared, and were here before us. They will be here after us - if we let them.

These shared resources are **the commons**: the inheritance of everything that lives. The commons is a description of physical reality. It predates every person, group, and institution, and none can own it.

Some commons are built. Energy grids, water systems, communication networks, and other systems created through collective effort become commons when they are essential and shared. Built commons follow the same principles as natural commons.

---

## The Values

### The commons is defined by the resource, not by people.

A watershed, a forest, an aquifer, a stretch of coastline - these have physical boundaries. The boundaries of a commons follow the resource. The boundaries of a commons follow the resource, independent of political, group, or federation boundaries.

### Sustainability is a physical fact.

The amount a resource can sustain is determined by reality. People, groups, and institutions determine how to share what is available. They cannot determine that more is available than exists.

### The commons is alive and changeable.

Human action changes the commons. Pollution degrades it, care restores it. Its capacity can grow or shrink depending on how it is treated.

### Responsibility is a condition of use.

Anyone who draws from the commons is responsible for its condition. The commons is a living system to be maintained.

### Future generations are stakeholders.

Decisions that deplete the commons beyond its ability to recover take from people who have no voice in the decision.

### Other living things are stakeholders.

The ecosystems that sustain the commons - forests, wetlands, species that maintain balance - are structural. Their needs are constraints on use.

### Stewardship is responsibility.

Built commons require defined roles with delegated authority to maintain, repair, and operate them. Stewardship is a responsibility carried on behalf of the community. Stewards are accountable to the stakeholders of the commons.

### Knowledge of the commons is itself a commons.

The expertise, methods, and understanding needed to sustain a commons - natural or built - belongs to all stakeholders and must be freely shared.
