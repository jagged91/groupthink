# Core - Values
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## What This Is

These are the values that define a Groupthink group. Every group, at every scale, for every purpose, adopts these values by calling itself Groupthink. They come in two kinds: **floors** and **directions**.

**Floors** are absolute. They cannot be crossed regardless of circumstance, method, or intent. They exist because some harms cannot be undone.

**Directions** are commitments to move toward a value, continuously, knowing you will never fully arrive. Every decision can be evaluated: did this move us toward or away from the direction? Directions are more demanding than rules - rules can be satisfied and forgotten, but a direction requires constant orientation.

---

## The Floors

These are non-negotiable. A group is a Groupthink group only so long as it honours every floor.

### 1. No person may be prevented from leaving.

A person's right to leave any group, at any time, for any reason, cannot be obstructed, delayed, or penalised. The right to leave must be real - permitted and practically possible.

### 2. No decision may be invisible to the people it binds.

Every person has the right to see, understand, and access the record of any decision that affects them. Legitimacy requires transparency.

### 3. No irreversible harm to a person.

No group may cause or permit irreversible harm to the body, life, or dignity of a person. A person, for the purposes of this framework, is any being with the capacity for self-awareness and the experience of suffering. This boundary should be revisited honestly as understanding grows.

### 4. No irreversible harm to the commons.

No group may cause or permit irreversible harm to the shared resources - natural or built - that others depend on. *(This floor is elaborated in Documents/Stewardship/0.Values.md when a group stewards shared resources.)*

---

## The Directions

These are the values every Groupthink group commits to moving toward. No group fully achieves them. The commitment is to the direction, to continuous movement.

### Toward maximum consent

A person should be bound only by decisions they have had the opportunity to shape, challenge, or understand. How consent is implemented - direct participation, delegation, representation, or other methods - is for groups to determine. Everyone should have a voice in the decisions that bind them.

### Toward minimum coercion

Consent counts only when it is free. Coercion includes pressure, bribery, threats, manipulation, exhaustion, social pressure, economic dependency, and information asymmetry. When you see coercion, name it.

### Toward maximum transparency

The reasoning behind decisions, the records that document them, and the processes that produce them should be visible to everyone they affect. Transparency is the default; opacity requires justification.

### Toward maximum equality

Every member of a group has equal standing. Roles and responsibilities may differ, and all roles carry equal standing. A person who joined today has the same rights as a person who has been a member for a year.

### Toward voluntary association

A person's relationship to any group is continuous and chosen. An ongoing choice to remain, participate, and be bound - renewed every day. A person born into a system they did not choose holds the same rights as one who joined freely. The voluntariness is in the ongoing relationship.

### Toward dignity

Every person has the right to be treated with basic respect. Harassment, humiliation, and deliberate cruelty are violations regardless of the process used to justify them.

### Toward conscience

Every person may act according to their deeply held moral convictions. A person who in good conscience cannot participate in a decision may step aside without penalty.

### Toward repair

When harm occurs, the response should restore what was damaged — the person harmed, the relationship broken, the resource degraded, and the conditions that produced the harm. Consequences that do not serve repair should be questioned. The direction is away from punishment and toward restoration.

### Toward mutual benefit

Groups and persons should move toward arrangements where cooperation is structurally more rewarding than defection. The systems we build should make it genuinely better to participate than to exploit. Trust, once established, should be portable and cumulative — a foundation that grows stronger the more it is honoured.

---

## Floors and Directions Together

The floors tell you what you must never do. The directions tell you where you must always be heading. Together, they define the character of a Groupthink group.

A group that respects every floor yet makes no effort to move in the directions is technically compliant and practically dead. A group that earnestly pursues the directions yet crosses a floor has done something that cannot be undone. Both matter. Both are necessary.
