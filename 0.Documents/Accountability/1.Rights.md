# Accountability - Rights
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## What This Is

These are the rights that apply when a group holds persons or groups accountable for harm. They build on the Core rights (Documents/Core/1.Rights.md) and address the specific dangers that arise when a community exercises power over one of its members or over another group.

---

## Rights of the Person or Group Facing Accountability

### 1. The right to structural examination

Before consequences are determined, the structural conditions that contributed to the harm must be examined. What pressures, scarcities, exclusions, or broken systems played a role? This examination is recorded and informs the response.

### 2. The right to be heard

No accountability action may proceed without the person or group having full opportunity to present their account, challenge the evidence, and propose their own path to repair. This right cannot be waived by the community.

### 3. The right to independent review

Every accountability finding must be reviewed by people with no stake in the outcome — ideally from outside the group. The review examines: was the harm real? Was the structural examination adequate? Is the proposed consequence proportional? Does the restoration path make repair genuinely possible?

### 4. The right to a defined restoration path

Every accountability action must include explicit conditions for restoration. What must the person or group do to repair the harm? What does restored standing look like? If the community cannot define a restoration path, it must say so openly and justify why — and the finding is subject to additional independent review.

### 5. The right to continued essentials

A person facing accountability retains full access to essentials — shelter, food, water, safety — regardless of the outcome. When a providing group exercises accountability, the Provision protections apply in full. *(See Documents/Provision/1.Rights.md.)*

### 6. The right to challenge the finding

A person or group may challenge an accountability finding by requesting review from a broader set of independent reviewers — from outside the group, from outside the federation, from people with relevant expertise. The challenge and its outcome are recorded.

### 7. The right to expiry

No structured separation is permanent without periodic reauthorization. Every separation has a defined duration. At expiry, the community must actively decide to renew — separation does not continue by default. The burden is on the community to justify renewal, not on the separated party to justify return.

### 8. Protection from disproportionate consequence

The consequence must be proportional to the harm. Structured separation for a minor harm is disproportionate. Network-standing consequences for a harm that does not involve a floor violation is disproportionate. Proportionality is reviewed independently.

---

## Rights of the Person or Group Harmed

### 9. The right to repair

A person or group that has been harmed has the right to have the harm acknowledged, the causes examined, and restoration pursued. This right exists whether or not the harming party cooperates.

### 10. The right to be heard

The harmed party's account of what happened and what repair would mean to them is central to the accountability process. Their voice carries weight in defining what restoration looks like.

### 11. The right to safety during the process

The harmed party is protected from retaliation, pressure, or further harm during the accountability process. If the harming party and the harmed party are in the same group, the group must ensure the harmed party's safety and comfort throughout.

---

## Rights of the Community

### 12. The right to transparency

The full accountability process — the harm identified, the structural examination, the finding, the consequence, and the restoration path — is recorded and available to all members and affected parties.

### 13. The right to structured separation

When a person or group has caused harm and does not voluntarily make repair, the community has the right to withdraw specific relationships, access, and cooperation — subject to the safeguards in this document.

### 14. The right to publish findings of floor violations

When an independent review confirms that a group has crossed a floor, the community has the right — and the responsibility — to publish the finding to the broader network. The publication includes the harm, the evidence, the review process, and the defined restoration path.

---

*This is the rights document for Account. These rights apply in addition to the Core rights whenever a group exercises its accountability function.*
