# Provision - Rights
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## What This Is

These are the rights that apply when a group provides essential needs to its members. They build on the Core rights (Documents/Core/1.Rights.md) and address the specific power dynamics that arise when people depend on a group for survival.

---

## Rights of Members in Providing Groups

### 1. The right to genuine exit

When a group provides essentials, the right to leave requires more than permission to depart. The group must ensure that leaving preserves a person's access to shelter, food, water, safety, and livelihood. This may require transition periods, portable resources, or active connection to alternatives.

### 2. The right to alternatives

A member of a providing group has the right to know what alternatives exist and to access them. The group has a responsibility to maintain awareness of alternatives and to share this information openly.

### 3. No conditional provision

Essentials are provided unconditionally. A person who dissents, stands aside, or is in dispute with the group retains full access to essentials.

### 4. The right to raise structural concerns

Any member can raise the concern that the group has become a trap - that dependency has compromised voluntariness. The group must examine it and record its response.

### 5. Protection during association disputes and accountability

If a group is considering ending its association with a member (Documents/Core/1.Rights.md §16), and the group provides essentials to that member, the group must ensure continued access to essentials during and after the process. Access to survival continues regardless of association status. This protection applies with equal force during accountability proceedings — when structured separation is imposed, access to essentials is unconditional. *(See Documents/Accountability/1.Rights.md §5.)*

---

## Rights of Providing Groups in Federation

### 6. Essential resource protection

When an inter-group arrangement coordinates access to resources that member groups need to survive, the arrangement must ensure that the right to leave remains real. If leaving would cut a group off from essentials, the arrangement has become coercive. This is a structural violation.

### 7. Redundancy as a right

Groups in arrangements that coordinate essential resources have the right to build and maintain independent access to those resources. No arrangement may prohibit a group from developing alternatives.

---

*This is the rights document for Provide. These rights apply in addition to the Core rights whenever a group supplies essential needs.*
