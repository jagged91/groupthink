# Provision - Values
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## When This Applies

A group **provides** when it supplies essential needs to its members - shelter, food, water, energy, healthcare, livelihood, or safety. The moment a group becomes a source of things its members need to survive, the stakes of membership change fundamentally, and additional values apply.

These values build on the Core (Documents/Core/0.Values.md) and extend it.

---

## The Principle

Voluntary association is only voluntary when leaving is survivable. When a group provides essentials, it holds structural power over its members - dependency creates power whether anyone intends it or not.

This is a structural condition that must be actively managed. The values below exist to ensure that provision serves its members and preserves their freedom.

---

## The Values

### Dependency is structural

When a group provides essentials, the power asymmetry is real regardless of anyone's intentions. Good people running a good group can still create a trap if the structure allows it. The safeguards must be structural, not reliant on goodwill.

### Alternatives must exist

A group that provides essentials has a responsibility to ensure that its members have genuine alternatives - other sources of the essentials they need. This responsibility extends to the broader systems the group exists within. If no alternative exists, the group must acknowledge this and work actively to create or connect to alternatives.

### Departure must remain real

The Core floor - no person may be prevented from leaving - becomes structurally critical when a group provides essentials. Leaving must mean continued access to shelter, food, water, and safety. This may require transition support, portable benefits, or connections to alternative providers.

### Provision is a function

A group that provides essentials serves its members through that provision. A person who receives food from a group retains full standing and autonomy. Provision creates responsibility for the group, and dignity for the person.

### Self-sufficiency is a direction

Over time, a providing group should move toward helping its members develop independent capacity. The direction is toward resilience - toward a world where each person has multiple sources and genuine choice.
