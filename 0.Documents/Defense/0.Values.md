# Defense - Values
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## When This Applies

A group **defends** when it protects against irreversible harm - to persons or to the commons. This is the only function in the Groupthink framework where the use of force may be legitimate.

Most groups will never need this function. It activates when the stakes involve harms that cannot be undone: violence against persons, destruction of ecosystems, poisoning of shared resources, acts that permanently diminish the commons or end a life.

These values build on the Core (Documents/Core/0.Values.md) and extend it.

---

## The Principle

The Groupthink framework relies on voluntary association, reputation, and exit as its primary mechanisms for resolving disputes and addressing bad behaviour. These mechanisms work when harms are recoverable - when the injured party can survive, adapt, and the system can self-correct.

These mechanisms reach their limit when harm is irreversible. A poisoned river cannot wait for reputational consequences. A murdered person cannot exercise their right to leave. When the harm cannot be undone, the framework must have a capacity to prevent or stop it - as a defensive function activated by specific, irreversible threats.

Enforcement exists only where soft mechanisms reach their limit - and soft mechanisms reach their limit only where harm is permanent.

---

## The Values

### Irreversibility is the threshold

The only harms that justify defensive force are irreversible ones. Fraud, breach of agreement, social harm, reputational damage - these are real harms, and they are recoverable. The system handles them through reputation, exit, and dispute resolution. Defense activates only when something cannot be undone.

### Defense is a function

Defense is a capacity that activates in response to specific threats and deactivates when the threat is resolved. The people who defend carry authority only within the specific defense action.

### Defense is accountable to the community

Defensive action is authorised by the community, accountable to the community, and reviewed by the community after the fact. No individual or subgroup has independent authority to use force.

### Defense is proportional

The response must be proportional to the threat. The minimum force necessary to prevent irreversible harm. No more.

### Defense preserves equality

The people who carry out defensive action carry the same standing as everyone else. Defensive roles should rotate where practical. The role should be burdensome enough that no one wants to keep it.

### Defense is transparent

Every defensive action is recorded in full - the threat identified, the authorization given, the action taken, the outcome, and the review. Every defensive action is visible to all.

---

## The Boundaries of Defense

Defense is specifically scoped. It covers:

- Prevention of irreversible harm to persons or the commons.

It does **not** extend to:

- Enforcing compliance with group decisions. *(Reputation, exit, and dispute resolution serve that purpose.)*
- Responding to past behaviour. *(Defense prevents future harm; accountability for the past follows other paths.)*
- Contested use of group resources. *(Negotiation and the group's decision process resolve these.)*
- Compelling continued association. *(Voluntary association is unconditional.)*
