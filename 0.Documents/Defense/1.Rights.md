# Defense - Rights
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## What This Is

These are the rights that apply when a group defends against irreversible harm. They build on the Core rights (Documents/Core/1.Rights.md) and address the specific dangers that arise when force enters the framework.

---

## Rights of Persons Under Defense

### 1. The right to safety

Every person has the right to be protected from irreversible harm - to their body, their life, and their capacity for self-determination. This right is inherent and stands above any group decision.

### 2. The right to due process

No defensive action may be taken against a person without: identification of the specific threat, authorization by the community, and opportunity for the person to be heard - except where immediate action is necessary to prevent imminent irreversible harm. When immediate action is taken, review must follow as soon as the immediate threat is resolved.

### 3. The right to proportionality

No person may be subjected to force beyond what is necessary to prevent the specific irreversible harm identified.

### 4. The right to review

Every person affected by a defensive action has the right to a full review of that action - by people with no stake in the outcome. The review examines: was the threat real? Was the authorization proper? Was the response proportional? Was the outcome just?

### 5. Protection from retaliation

A person who raises a concern about a defensive action - whether they believe it was unjustified, disproportionate, or improperly authorized - is protected from retaliation.

---

## Rights of the Commons Under Defense

### 6. Standing to defend

When irreversible harm to the commons is occurring or imminent, any stakeholder has standing to invoke defense - to trigger the defensive function directly (beyond invoking the commons per Documents/Stewardship/1.Rights.md §2).

### 7. Precautionary standing

When the evidence of irreversible harm is uncertain but the potential harm is catastrophic, the burden of proof shifts. The party proposing the action bears the burden of demonstrating safety.

---

## Rights of the Community

### 8. The right to disarm

The community retains the right to end any defensive capacity, recall any person acting in a defensive role, and review any defensive action. Defense exists at the community's pleasure, not its own.

### 9. The right to refuse

Every person may decline to participate in a defensive action according to their conscience.

### 10. The right to mutual defense

Groups in a mutual defense compact have the right to invoke the compact when they face a threat of irreversible harm beyond their individual capacity to resist. The invocation triggers the compact's pre-defined process (standing decisions), requires independent confirmation that the threat is real and imminent, and each member group retains the right to decline participation by conscience. *(See Documents/Federation/2.Implementation.md § Mutual Defense Compacts.)*

---

*This is the rights document for Defend. These rights apply in addition to the Core rights whenever a group exercises its defensive function.*
