# Defense - Implementation
### Groupthink - Version 0.6

---

## What This Is

This document offers starting points for how a group can exercise its defensive function - the capacity to prevent irreversible harm to persons or the commons. This is the most dangerous function in the framework. Every suggestion below is designed to constrain it.

---

## Activation

Defense activates in response to a specific, identified threat of irreversible harm and deactivates when the threat is resolved.

### Triggering defense

1. Any member or stakeholder can raise a defense concern - the claim that irreversible harm is occurring or imminent.
2. The concern must identify the specific harm, the evidence for it, and why it is irreversible.
3. The group examines the concern. If the threat is confirmed as irreversible and imminent, the defensive function activates.
4. If the threat is not imminent, the group deliberates before activating. Careful deliberation serves proportionality.

### Emergency action

When irreversible harm is actively occurring (a person is being harmed, a resource is being destroyed), any member may act immediately to prevent the harm. This is the only circumstance where action precedes authorization. Emergency action must be:

1. The minimum necessary to stop the immediate harm.
2. Reported to the group immediately after.
3. Reviewed by the group as soon as possible.

Emergency action that exceeds the minimum necessary, or that is taken when the threat was not genuinely imminent, is itself a violation.

---

## Authorization

All non-emergency defensive action requires community authorization.

1. The threat is identified and described to the community.
2. The proposed response is described - what will be done, by whom, with what limits.
3. The community deliberates. Consent is sought through the group's normal decision process.
4. The authorization is specific: it names the threat, the permitted response, the people authorized to act, and the conditions under which the authorization expires.
5. Every authorization has an expiry. Open-ended authority is incompatible with this framework.

---

## Conduct

Defensive action is constrained:

1. **Proportionality.** The response must be proportional to the threat. The minimum force necessary to prevent the irreversible harm. No more.
2. **Specificity.** Action is directed at the specific threat, targeted to the source of harm.
3. **Transparency.** Every action is recorded in real time where possible, or as soon as possible after.
4. **Prevention only.** Defense prevents harm. Once the threat is resolved, the defensive action ends. Accountability for past behaviour follows other paths.
5. **Scoped authority.** People acting in a defensive role carry authority only within the specific action authorized. Their standing returns to normal when the action concludes.

---

## Review

Every defensive action is reviewed after the fact.

1. The review is conducted by people with no stake in the outcome - ideally from outside the group.
2. The review examines: Was the threat real? Was the authorization proper? Was the response proportional? Was the outcome just?
3. The review's findings are recorded and available to all members and affected parties.
4. If the review finds the action was unjustified or disproportionate, the group must acknowledge this and make whatever repair is possible.

---

## Rotation and Constraint

To prevent the defensive function from becoming a power structure:

1. Defensive roles should rotate. No person should serve in a defensive capacity indefinitely.
2. The role carries the same standing as any other role - same voice, same compensation, same standing.
3. The community can end any defensive capacity at any time (Documents/Defense/1.Rights.md §8).
4. Periodic review of whether the defensive function is still needed. If the threat has passed, the function deactivates.

---

## The Danger

This function is the most dangerous in the framework. History shows that defensive institutions - militias, police, armies - tend to become the very threat they were created to address. Every safeguard in this document exists because of that history.

A group that activates defense should treat it as a wound - necessary sometimes, and always to be healed as quickly as possible. The goal is always to return to a state where soft mechanisms - reputation, exit, dispute resolution - are sufficient.
