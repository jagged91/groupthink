# Document One - Rights of Persons
### Groupthink - Version 0.4

---

## Why This Exists

You can belong to something and still be free inside it. These are the rights that make that possible. They exist within the commons (Document Zero) — the shared physical world whose limits apply to everyone. Within those limits, these rights cannot be overridden. Any group can customise its rules, but it can never violate these articles.

---

## The Articles

### Article Zero - Voluntary Association

Your participation in any group is always your choice. You choose when to join. You choose when to leave. You can always change your mind.

Every person has the right to:

1. **Choose** which groups you belong to. Membership is always a personal choice.
2. **Leave** any group, at any time, for any reason.
3. **Start something new** — leave and create a new group with anyone who wants to join you.
4. **Say no** — refuse to join any group, refuse to participate in any decision, refuse any obligation.
5. **Stay** — a member cannot be removed against their will. If a group has a problem with a member, it can raise discussions, form smaller groups, or split — but it cannot remove someone's membership.

**No one is in charge of you unless you say so, and you can change your mind.**

*The commons (D0) limits what groups and federations can decide, but it does not limit your right to associate, leave, or say no. Voluntary association applies to every human institution built within the commons.*

### Article One - Equal Power

Every member can raise concerns and shape decisions. The right to participate, the right to be heard, and the right to leave belong to every member equally and permanently.

### Article Two - Open Decisions

Groups make decisions openly. Everything is written down — every discussion, every outcome, every person who joined or left. This is the record. Any member can keep a copy. If there is a disagreement about what was decided, the record is the answer. What the group decides together is final. Rules only apply going forward.

### Article Three - Fractal Structure

Groups can create smaller groups inside themselves (circles) to handle specific topics. Circles follow their own rules but must respect the parent group's rules. Groups can also split — any members can leave and form their own group. This right is unconditional.

### Article Four - No Coercion

Consent only counts if it is free. No one should be pressured, bribed, threatened, or trapped into agreeing — or into staying silent.

A group that controls things its members need to survive holds power over those members. Groups should avoid becoming the only source of things their members need. The right to leave, the right to block, and the right to say no must be livable.

If you see coercion, name it. That is every member's responsibility.

---

## Changing This Document

1. This document follows its own rules. If people want to change it, they raise a discussion and decide together.
2. A group can choose to follow a specific version of this document.
3. **Article Zero can never be changed.**
4. **The Articles can never be violated**, but their wording can be clarified through the normal decision-making process.

---

*This is Document One. It establishes the rights of every person. These rights exist within the commons (Document Zero) and limit all governance built above them (Documents Two and Three). If any higher document and this document ever conflict, this document prevails.*
